­Electrical and Computer Engineering Partnerships (ECEP)

The Electrical and Computer Engineering ­Department has been forging new partnerships with industry over the past year with its ­Electrical and Computer Engineering Partnerships (ECEP) ­program. The program tightly ­couples ­academic education and research with the needs of industry by ­working ­directly with ­partners in nurturing our pipelines of talent both at the undergraduate and graduate levels. We enhance ­visibility to our student activities with ­industry through open houses and ­research reviews, and we propagate ­industry needs with our students through hands-on ­projects, material in courses, ­industry lecturers, and collaborative ­research opportunities. ECEP also provides linkages for partners to access UCLA’s state-of-the-art facilities. These ­activities are ­possible through the support of our ­partners. More details are available at the ECEP website: https://www.ee.ucla.edu/industry.

Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship

The Qualcomm Innovation Fellowship began in 2009, and has continued to grow with the addition of more universities, more candidates, and expansion to research centers internationally. Qualcomm believes that research and development is the key to harnessing the power of imagination and to discovering new possibilities. Qualcomm’s core values of innovation, execution and teamwork is embedded in its Fellowship program. The goal is to enable students to pursue their futuristic innovative ideas.
